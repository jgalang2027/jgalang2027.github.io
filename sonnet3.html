LITERATURE STUDY - ENGLISH |Gian Quirsten C. Ramirez|

SONNET #3

PETRARCHAN

A Tale of Man’s Heart Unmoved

To the corner where I first glimpsed your face

In my sleep, I’ll linger, unmoving, no regret

Tales of love, your portrait on my hand, all set

Should your cross path with this maiden, tell her my place

Misunderstood for monetary, this is not the case

Not penniless, just that love’s a silhouette

Logic falters when emotions beset

How can I go on when you still occupy my heart’s space?

When dawn unravels, and you long for me

In your heart’s quest, where do I reside?

Perhaps you’ll revisit the place where we first see

To our meeting haven, where I started to love thee

On the street’s corner where memories coincide

With firm resolve, unmoved, I choose to be

SHAKESPEAREAN

Devotion Unshaken

Returning to where our love took a stand

In my sleeping bag, my stay shall be braced

Verses echo, with your portrait in my hand

If thou find this woman, convey where I'm placed

In wealth they extend, misconstrued goal

Not in despair, but love's like a web

Puzzled emotions, heart fallen in a hole

Can't move on, love will not ebb

If you awaken to a day missing our past

Your heart ponders, seeking stories untold

Contemplating, eager of our love to last

I'll just be at the corner where love's secrets hold

In stillness I stay, love's undying song,

Echoes of devotion, forever strong.
